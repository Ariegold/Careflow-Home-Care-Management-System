Software Analysis

#3

HHA Exchange

Legacy Platform

Overview

A widely-used home care software platform offering robust features for EVV compliance, employee and patient management, authorisation tracking, and charting. One of the most established platforms in the market.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive EVV compliance capabilities
  • Widely recognised — minimal learning curve for new hires
  • Supports integration with third-party software systems
  • Allows external EVV data feeds from other platforms
  • Employee and patient management functionality

Weaknesses

  • Antiquated, dated user interface
  • "Clunky" user experience compared to modern solutions
  • Lack of significant system updates to match contemporary standards
  • Not as user-friendly as newer platforms

Best Use Cases

Agencies prioritising industry-standard EVV compliance and those seeking a platform with widespread familiarity amongst potential staff hires. Suitable for agencies where insurance companies mandate HHA Exchange for EVV purposes.

#2

WellSky

Skilled Care Focus

Overview

A comprehensive home healthcare management platform, particularly well-suited for agencies providing skilled nursing services. Offers tools for OASIS assessments, nursing visits, and PT/OT management alongside personal care functionality.

Strengths

  • Strong skilled nursing and clinical visit tools
  • OASIS assessment capability for Medicare clients
  • Comprehensive approach to home healthcare management
  • Widely used by skilled home care agencies
  • High user ratings and survey responses

Weaknesses

  • Scheduling tool described as "clunky"
  • Difficulty tracking authorisation-to-visit workflows
  • Requires navigating away to view subsequent patient schedules
  • High activation cost ($2,000–$5,000)
  • Long-term contracts with significant cancellation fees
  • Reported lack of mobile access (personal care variant)

Best Use Cases

Agencies primarily delivering skilled nursing, PT/OT, and Medicare-funded services. Best suited for established agencies with budget capacity for higher activation costs and long-term software commitment.

Risks & Limitations

The contractual lock-in period (2–3 years) presents significant financial risk for start-up agencies. Transitioning away incurs substantial cancellation fees. The scheduling workflow is inefficient for coordinating large numbers of concurrent visits.

Industry Observations

EVV as Industry Standard

Electronic Visit Verification is described as both a legal requirement and a compliance cornerstone. Insurance companies are mandating specific platforms (e.g., HHA Exchange) for EVV data submission.

Software Market Fragmentation

No single platform was identified as fully meeting all operational needs across home care, skilled nursing, and medical staffing. Operators frequently use multiple systems, creating inefficiency.

Documentation Standards Gap

Many agencies still rely on manual documentation methods (notepads, Microsoft Word). The transition to digital care documentation platforms remains incomplete across the industry.

Cost Barriers for Start-ups

Activation fees of $2,000–$5,000 and long-term contractual commitments create significant barriers to entry for new agency operators.

Compliance Complexity

Agencies face multi-layered compliance requirements including employee document tracking, authorisation validation, overtime restrictions, and care plan adherence.

Mobile Access Expectation

Mobile accessibility for caregivers (documentation via mobile phone) is increasingly expected as standard functionality. Platforms lacking mobile access may be deemed unsuitable.

Operational Recommendations

01

Evaluate software against all service lines before committing

Agencies operating or planning to operate across multiple service types (home care, skilled nursing, medical staffing) should prioritise platforms that support all business verticals from a single system.

02

Scrutinise contractual terms and exit clauses

Before committing to any platform, carefully review contract duration, cancellation fees, and total cost of ownership. Start-up agencies should favour platforms with flexible terms.

03

Implement the six documentation attributes as standard practice

Adopt accuracy, accessibility, comprehensiveness, consistency, timeliness, and relevancy as the quality framework for all clinical documentation within the agency.

04

Ensure authorisation validation is enforced by the software

Implement hard stops preventing scheduling of visits beyond valid authorisation periods to eliminate revenue leakage from unbillable services.

05

Build overtime safeguards into scheduling workflows

Automated overtime restrictions should be in place to prevent caregiver over-scheduling, particularly where overtime services are not reimbursed by insurance companies.

06

Standardise client home folders with essential documentation

Every client's home folder should include: timesheets, the agreed service/care plan, rights and responsibilities documentation, DNR instructions (where applicable), and allergy information.

07

Prioritise mobile-accessible documentation systems

Given the field-based nature of home care work, ensure the selected software platform provides full mobile functionality for caregivers conducting documentation at the point of care.
